ACT-1: Background

L85A2

At the 2004 Gun Show in Las Vegas, USA on February 11, HK displayed a series of carbine guns collectively known as "HKM4". This weapon is actually a modification of the M16 series rifles. The HKM4 development program began in 2002. When HK improved the British SA80 (L85/L86) weapon system, it began to evaluate the technical feasibility of improving M16 series carbine guns in early 2002. The purpose of the study was to improve the reliability and service life of M16 series weapons.

ACT-2: Technical Features

HK416 With Desert Application

HK416 is characterized by maintaining strong firepower while maintaining the stability of guns. The intake system of HK416 assault rifle ensures that propellant gas and residue do not enter the interior of the gun body, reduces the influence of dirt on the gun, and improves the reliability of the weapon. The HK416 barrel has a long life and can fire 20,000 rounds of ammunition with little reduction in accuracy. In addition, the gun is not afraid of flooding or dry climate.

HK416

To improve the service life, HK manufactures four kinds of cold forged barrels of different lengths, including 10, 14.5, 16.5 and 20 inches, with high quality European steel. The barrel life is more than 20,000 rounds, and there are four kinds of barrels of different lengths. In addition, a new buffer device is used to improve the reliability and safety of the weapon. In addition, more reliable magazines have been manufactured.

ACT4: Something Interesting

Since Colt intended to sue HK for infringement of their rights in 2004, HK abandoned the name HKM4 and renamed it HK416. HK416 was officially launched in February 2005. At the same time, a new structure of Picatini rail guard was adopted, which made the appearance of HK416 more distinct from the original M16/M4.
